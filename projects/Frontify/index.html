Equinix Design Language

Equinix is a global company with multiple digital products that are being created by multiple teams. In order to maintain a consistent brand image, it is important to provide guidelines that designers can reference when creating new designs or redesigning an existing flow.

The Equinix User Experience team decided to use an online tool called Frontify to create a Style Guide that lives online, accessible by anyone at anytime. Using a combination of an online style guide and a sketch toolkit for wireframe and high-res design, we are hoping to make the process of making consistent design more easy and streamlined.

Online Style Guide (Frontify)

VIEW GUIDE

The online style guide is divided into three sections: Visual Framework, UI Component Library, and Content Style Guide. The three sections work together to show how an element should look, how it should be coded, and what the wording and content should be in that element.

I worked mainly on the Visual Framework section of the Style Guide. The Visual Framework is constructed based on (but not strictly following) the concept of Atomic Design—the most basic design elements are atoms, these atoms come together to create molecules, building up into a page/layout. Since Equinix products are very heavy on forms and datagrids, the categorization of the elements was focused on such elements.

For each of the elements, the page details its specifications, styling, and usage. Specifications are like a skeleton—they define the structure of the element. Then, through styling, a skin is put on. According to the situation, the skeleton can have a different "outfit." The usage section then lists main examples of the element being used, along with some content tips.

Sketch Toolkit

Having solid guidelines online is great, but the information is very thorough and not really necessary for everyday use. For an everyday design task, being able to quickly grab a pre-made asset (a.k.a. "symbols") and drop it into the design saves a lot of time.
